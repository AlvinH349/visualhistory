Welcome to Times Square!

Times Square didn't always have that name. It was originally known as Longacre Square, until 1904 when The New York Times moved its headquarters to the newly built Times Building.

The very first New Year's Eve ball drop in Times Square was held on December 31, 1907. The ball was built from iron and wood, whereas today it is made with crystal triangles.

Since 2018, reportedly over 26 million people visit Times Square each year! No wonder it's sometimes referred to as "The Crossroads of the World"!
